The Shanty Is Whole

sung over a fully inked sea

PRIVATEER

a living map

This sea does not exist until someone sails it. Beyond your harbour the chart is blank vellum — but an unseen cartographer follows your bow, inking the world as you find it.

An old song is scattered across these waters: a verse in every port, one drowned in a wreck, one kept by the thing drawn in the margin. Gather all five, and the sea will sing.
